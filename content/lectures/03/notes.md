# Lecture 03: The Atmosphere and Climate

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 7  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Builds the atmospheric side of climate: composition, greenhouse effect, and heat transfer.

## Lecture Goals

- Know basic atmospheric properties.
- Explain how the atmosphere changes Earth energy balance.
- Describe how energy is moved around the planet.

## High-Yield Content

- The atmosphere affects incoming shortwave and outgoing longwave radiation.
- Greenhouse gases warm the surface by absorbing and re-emitting infrared radiation.
- Albedo controls how much incoming radiation is reflected rather than absorbed.
- Atmospheric circulation redistributes energy from low to high latitudes.
- Climate is an outcome of energy input, atmospheric composition, circulation, and feedbacks.

## Study Targets

- Master greenhouse effect vs. albedo vs. circulation as distinct mechanisms.
- Know how CO2 matters both in modern climate and deep-time carbon-cycle lectures.
- Review diagrams of atmospheric circulation and energy budget.

## Active Recall

- How does albedo change climate sensitivity?
- Why does atmospheric circulation matter for regional environments?
