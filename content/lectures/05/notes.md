# Lecture 05: Earth Topography: Plate Tectonics

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 6  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Explains the physical engine behind topography, continent/ocean distribution, and long-term environmental change.

## Lecture Goals

- Know Earth internal structure at the level needed for plate tectonics.
- Explain how plate boundaries generate topography and geologic activity.
- Connect tectonics to climate, sea level, habitats, and paleogeography.

## High-Yield Content

- Earth is layered, with lithospheric plates moving over deeper mantle.
- Divergent, convergent, and transform boundaries produce different landforms and hazards.
- Plate motion controls mountain building, ocean basins, volcanic activity, and continental positions.
- Tectonics affects climate through paleogeography, weathering, volcanism, and ocean gateways.
- Supercontinent assembly and breakup change sea level, habitats, and evolutionary pathways.

## Study Targets

- Be able to identify boundary types and expected topography.
- Know why plate tectonics is a first-order control on environments.
- Link tectonics to L12 paleogeography and L20-L23 extinction/climate history.

## Active Recall

- How do different plate boundaries create different environmental settings?
- Why does continental position matter for climate?
- How can tectonics contribute to mass extinctions or radiations?

- L13 includes geosphere controls in Earth-system models.
- L20 and L21 use paleogeography and volcanism in extinction explanations.
