## Role In The Course

Sets the course frame: environments are dynamic, controlled by physical and biological processes, and reconstructed through scientific reasoning.

## Lecture Goals

- Explain what counts as an Earth surface environment.
- Understand that the course is organized around present controls, past inference tools, and Earth-history transitions.
- Use the scientific method as the basic logic for interpreting geological evidence.

## High-Yield Content

- Surface environments include terrestrial, freshwater, marginal marine, and marine settings.
- The course asks why environments differ across space and why they changed across deep time.
- Physical controls include climate, topography, plate tectonics, and ocean/atmosphere systems.
- Biological controls include organisms as ecosystem engineers and drivers of biogeochemical change.
- Scientific claims should connect observation, hypothesis, prediction, test, and revision.

## Study Targets

- Know the course-scale story: present controls -> reconstruction tools -> historical transitions -> human impacts.
- Be able to name examples of environments and the factors that produce them.
- Use this lecture as the organizing map for the whole final.

## Active Recall

- What are the major categories of Earth surface environments?
- Why is environmental history not just a list of past events?
- How does the scientific method apply when geologists cannot directly observe ancient environments?
