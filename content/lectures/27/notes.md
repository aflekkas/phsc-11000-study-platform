# Lecture 27: Human Impacts on Climate

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 10  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Closes the course by applying energy-budget, atmosphere, carbon-cycle, and Earth-system logic to industrial climate change.

## Lecture Goals

- Understand industrial-era climate change.
- Know basic future climate-change logic.
- Know mitigation pathways at a conceptual level.

## High-Yield Content

- Industrial climate change is driven mainly by anthropogenic greenhouse gas emissions.
- Fossil fuel combustion returns ancient buried carbon to the active carbon cycle.
- Observed warming, CO2 rise, ocean heat uptake, ice melt, sea-level rise, and extreme changes fit greenhouse forcing expectations.
- Future climate depends on emissions, feedbacks, sensitivity, and mitigation choices.
- Mitigation reduces forcing through emissions cuts, energy changes, land-use practices, and carbon management.
