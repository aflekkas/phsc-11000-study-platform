# Lecture 25: Human Evolution

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 11  
Exam priority: Medium

## Role In The Course

Places humans within primate evolution, hominin divergence, and environmental change.

## Lecture Goals

- Know origin and evolution of primates.
- Understand divergence of the human lineage.
- Understand evolution of Homo.

## High-Yield Content

- Primates have anatomical and behavioral traits linked to arboreal life, vision, grasping, and sociality.
- Human lineage divergence involves bipedalism, changing habitats, and multiple hominin forms.
- Evolution of Homo includes changes in brain size, tools, diet, dispersal, and social/ecological flexibility.
- Human evolution occurred against changing late Cenozoic climates and environments.
- The lecture sets up humans as both products of environmental history and later drivers of environmental change.

## Study Targets

- Know broad sequence: primates -> apes -> hominins -> Homo -> modern humans.
- Avoid linear ladder thinking; multiple hominin lineages overlapped.
- Connect environmental variability to adaptation and dispersal.
