# Lecture 18: Colonization of the Land

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 11  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Covers land plants and animals as a major restructuring of surface environments.

## Lecture Goals

- Explain greening of the land.
- Explain colonization of land by animals.
- Connect terrestrialization to soils, weathering, carbon cycle, and habitats.

## High-Yield Content

- Land plants altered weathering, soil formation, runoff, carbon cycling, and atmospheric composition.
- Plant innovations include cuticles, vascular tissue, roots, spores/seeds, and structural support.
- Animals colonized land in multiple lineages, often following plant-created habitats and food webs.
- Terrestrialization changed both physical environments and evolutionary opportunity.
- The movement onto land was not a single event but a sequence of ecological expansions.

## Study Targets

- Know plant adaptations to land and their environmental consequences.
- Know broad animal colonization challenges: desiccation, support, reproduction, respiration.
- Link terrestrialization to later Carboniferous coal swamps.

## Active Recall

- How did plants change the physical Earth surface?
- What problems must organisms solve to live on land?
- How does terrestrialization connect biology to climate?
