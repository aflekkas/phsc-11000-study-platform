# Lecture 23: Cenozoic Events

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 10  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Covers environmental change after the end-Cretaceous, especially cooling and Pleistocene glaciation.

## Lecture Goals

- Know environmental change through the Cenozoic.
- Understand the Pleistocene Ice Age.
- Connect climate, tectonics, ocean circulation, and biotic change.

## High-Yield Content

- Cenozoic climate trends include long-term cooling from early greenhouse conditions toward icehouse conditions.
- Tectonics, weathering, ocean gateways, CO2, and orbital forcing all matter.
- Pleistocene glacial cycles involved ice sheets, sea-level change, habitats, and species distributions.
- Proxy records such as oxygen isotopes are crucial for reconstructing climate.
- Cenozoic environmental shifts frame mammal and human evolution.

## Study Targets

- Know greenhouse-to-icehouse trend and major controls.
- Be able to explain glacial/interglacial cycles in terms of forcing and feedback.
- Connect sea-level changes to habitats and migration routes.

## Active Recall

- Why did Cenozoic climate cool over time?
- How do oxygen isotopes help reconstruct ice volume or temperature?
- How did Pleistocene glaciation shape environments and evolution?
