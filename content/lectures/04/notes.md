# Lecture 04: The Ocean

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 9  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Adds oceans as climate regulators, habitats, and heat/chemical reservoirs.

## Lecture Goals

- Describe basic ocean properties.
- Distinguish major marine environments.
- Explain how oceans affect climate and energy transfer.

## High-Yield Content

- Oceans cover most of Earth surface and store large amounts of heat.
- Temperature, salinity, pressure, light, oxygen, and nutrients structure marine environments.
- Ocean circulation moves heat and materials across latitudes and depths.
- Marine environments include shelves, reefs, deep sea, pelagic zones, and benthic settings.
- Changes in sea level and circulation reshape habitats and extinction/recovery patterns.

## Study Targets

- Know the environmental differences between shallow shelf, reef, pelagic, and deep-sea settings.
- Be able to connect ocean circulation to climate and oxygen/anoxia.
- Review how marine settings appear again in labs and mass-extinction lectures.

## Active Recall

- Why are oceans such powerful climate buffers?
- What environmental gradients structure marine life?
- How can changes in sea level and circulation trigger biological consequences?
