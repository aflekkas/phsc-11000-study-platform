## Role In The Course

Continues Mesozoic restructuring through reptile diversification and the end-Cretaceous extinction.

## Lecture Goals

- Understand diversification of Mesozoic reptiles.
- Know the end-Cretaceous mass extinction.
- Connect predation, competition, convergence, and extinction/recovery.

## High-Yield Content

- Mesozoic reptiles diversified on land, in the air, and in the sea.
- Marine reptiles independently invaded similar niches, making convergence a key theme.
- End-Cretaceous extinction is linked strongly to bolide impact, with environmental effects including darkness, cooling, food-web collapse, and possible volcanism interaction.
- The extinction removed non-avian dinosaurs and many marine groups, opening later Cenozoic opportunities.
- Mesozoic-Cenozoic transition connects extinction selectivity to mammal and bird diversification.

## Study Targets

- Know major marine reptile groups and feeding/ecological roles at a broad level.
- Be able to explain convergent evolution using marine reptiles.
- Know end-Cretaceous causes and consequences.
