# Lecture 06: Ecosystem Engineering

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 5  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Shows that organisms do not just respond to environments; they also modify them.

## Lecture Goals

- Define ecosystem engineering.
- Identify ways organisms alter physical and chemical environments.
- Use biological feedbacks as part of Earth history explanations.

## High-Yield Content

- Ecosystem engineers change habitat structure, sediment dynamics, chemistry, and resource flows.
- Biological activity can create new niches and alter physical conditions.
- Reefs, burrowing, photosynthesis, land plants, and humans are major examples across the course.
- Biological innovations can become Earth-system forcing factors.
- This lecture is a bridge from present controls to major life-driven transitions.

## Study Targets

- Know examples: reef builders, burrowers, land plants, photosynthesizers, humans.
- Be ready to explain feedback loops where life changes environment and environment reshapes life.
- Use this concept in Cambrian, terrestrialization, coal swamp, and human-impact lectures.

## Active Recall

- What makes an organism an ecosystem engineer?
- How can ecosystem engineering produce evolutionary opportunity?
- Why is ecosystem engineering central to Earth environmental history?
