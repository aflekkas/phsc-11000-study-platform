## Role In The Course

Starts the end-Permian/Mesozoic story: extinction, recovery, predation escalation, and ecological replacement.

## Lecture Goals

- Understand the end-Permian mass extinction.
- Introduce the Mesozoic Marine Revolution.
- Understand post-extinction ecological opportunity.

## High-Yield Content

- End-Permian extinction was the largest Phanerozoic extinction and reset marine/terrestrial ecosystems.
- Likely mechanisms include massive volcanism, greenhouse warming, ocean anoxia/euxinia, acidification, and cascading stress.
- Post-Paleozoic marine communities differ profoundly from Paleozoic ones.
- The Mesozoic Marine Revolution involves increased predation, especially durophagy, and prey adaptations.
- Survivor groups and opportunists structured recovery and later diversification.
