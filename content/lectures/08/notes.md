# Lecture 08: Principles of Stratigraphy

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 4  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Gives the rules for ordering rocks and events before absolute dates are added.

## Lecture Goals

- Define uniformitarianism.
- Use basic stratigraphic principles.
- Apply those principles to reconstruct event sequences.

## High-Yield Content

- Uniformitarianism uses present processes as guides for interpreting past deposits, while allowing rates/intensities to vary.
- Superposition, original horizontality, lateral continuity, cross-cutting relationships, and inclusions help order events.
- Unconformities represent missing time or erosion/nondeposition.
- Stratigraphy is about sequence: what happened before what.
- These principles are the grammar of geologic history.

## Study Targets

- Practice ordering diagrams from oldest to youngest.
- Know common unconformity logic.
- Be able to explain how relative dating differs from absolute dating.
