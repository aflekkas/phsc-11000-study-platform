# Lecture 02: Earth Energy Budget: Insolation

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 7  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Introduces solar energy as the starting point for climate and surface temperature.

## Lecture Goals

- Define insolation and why solar radiation matters.
- Explain how incoming energy varies by latitude, season, orbital geometry, and time.
- Connect energy input to climate distribution.

## High-Yield Content

- Solar radiation is the dominant external energy source for Earth surface climate.
- Insolation depends on angle of incidence, day length, and distance from the Sun.
- Latitude and season produce uneven heating, which drives climate gradients.
- Temporal variation in insolation includes daily, seasonal, and orbital-scale change.
- Uneven heating sets up later topics: atmosphere/ocean energy transfer and climate feedbacks.

## Study Targets

- Be able to explain why the equator receives more effective solar energy than poles.
- Review diagrams showing angle of incidence, seasons, and radiation balance.
- Know what changes when insolation changes: temperature gradients, climate zones, ice extent, and feedbacks.

## Active Recall
