# Lecture 26: Human Impacts on Biological Communities

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 11  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Shifts from humans as evolved organisms to humans as major ecological agents.

## Lecture Goals

- Know major human impacts on biological communities.
- Understand mitigation strategies.
- Connect modern impacts to earlier course themes of extinction, invasion, and ecosystem engineering.

## High-Yield Content

- Human impacts include overkill/overharvesting, land-use change, habitat fragmentation, introductions/invasive species, pollution, and altered food webs.
- Extinction and community restructuring are not only deep-time phenomena.
- Introduced species can transform ecosystems by predation, competition, disease, and ecosystem engineering.
- Mitigation includes protected areas, restoration, regulation, invasive control, and conservation planning.
- Modern biodiversity change is a continuation of the course theme: life and environment interact dynamically.

## Study Targets

- Know overkill, land use, and introductions as distinct categories.
- Be able to compare modern extinctions with deep-time mass extinctions carefully.
- Use concrete examples if the lecture gives them.

## Active Recall

- What are the main ways humans alter biological communities?
- How are modern extinctions similar to and different from past mass extinctions?
- What makes mitigation difficult?
