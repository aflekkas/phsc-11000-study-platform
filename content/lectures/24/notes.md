# Lecture 24: Mammal Evolution

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 9  
Exam priority: Medium

## Role In The Course

Explains mammal history as shaped by chance, extinction, climate, and ecological opportunity.

## Lecture Goals

- Understand Mesozoic mammal evolution.
- Understand Cenozoic mammal evolution.
- Distinguish chance/extinction effects from climate/ecology effects.

## High-Yield Content

- Mammals originated before the Cenozoic but remained relatively constrained during much of the Mesozoic.
- End-Cretaceous extinction created ecological opportunity for mammal diversification.
- Cenozoic mammal evolution reflects both contingency and environmental change.
- Climate, vegetation, geography, and habitat openness shape mammal adaptations.
- Mammal evolution sets up primate and human evolution.

## Study Targets

- Know why mammals were not simply new after dinosaurs disappeared.
- Be able to explain adaptive radiation after extinction.
- Know climate/habitat links to mammalian forms at a broad level.

## Active Recall

- How did end-Cretaceous extinction affect mammals?
