Source PDF: `source.pdf`

## Role In The Course

Introduces stable isotopes as proxies for temperature, carbon cycling, and environmental change.

## Lecture Goals

- Define stable isotopes.
- Explain isotope fractionation.
- Know how environmental conditions affect isotope ratios.
- Understand chemostratigraphy.

## High-Yield Content

- Stable isotopes of the same element differ in neutron number, not chemical identity.
- Fractionation occurs because lighter and heavier isotopes behave slightly differently in physical, chemical, and biological processes.
- Oxygen isotopes can be used as paleotemperature and ice-volume proxies.
- Carbon isotopes help track organic carbon burial, carbon-cycle change, and environmental perturbations.
- Chemostratigraphy uses chemical signals to correlate rocks and infer change through time.

## Study Targets

- Know proxy logic: measured isotope value -> process/fractionation -> inferred environment.
- Be able to explain why proxies are indirect and uncertain.
- Review examples involving oxygen, carbon, temperature, ice, and organic burial.
