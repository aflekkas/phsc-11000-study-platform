Source PDF: `source.pdf`

## Role In The Course

Explains the fossil and environmental significance of rapid animal diversification near the Precambrian-Cambrian transition.

## Lecture Goals

- Know the fossil record of the Precambrian-Cambrian transition.
- Explain environmental significance of the Cambrian Radiation.
- Understand timing debates.

## High-Yield Content

- The Cambrian Radiation marks rapid appearance/diversification of many animal groups, especially skeletonized forms.
- Trace fossils, body fossils, skeletonization, predation, and burrowing all matter.
- Animals became major ecosystem engineers, changing sediments, food webs, and marine environments.
- Timing is debated because fossil evidence, molecular clocks, and preservation biases do not line up perfectly.
- Potential drivers include oxygen, ecology, predation, developmental/genetic changes, and environmental opportunity.
