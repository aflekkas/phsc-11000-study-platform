# Lecture 14: The Early Earth and the Origin of Life

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 10  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Begins the deep-time transitions block with early Earth conditions and earliest life evidence.

## Lecture Goals

- Describe early Earth conditions.
- Know the oldest evidence of life and why it is debated.
- Connect early life to later environmental transformation.

## High-Yield Content

- Early Earth differed strongly from today in atmosphere, oceans, crust, impact environment, and oxygen availability.
- Life-origin scenarios must account for prebiotic chemistry, energy sources, and habitable settings.
- Oldest-life evidence can include microfossils, stromatolites, isotopic signals, and biosignatures, but interpretation is difficult.
- The absence of free oxygen matters for early metabolism and preservation.
- Evidence quality matters: ancient rocks are altered and sparse.

## Study Targets

- Know types of evidence and why each can be ambiguous.
- Be able to explain why early Earth was not simply modern Earth plus time.
- Use this lecture to set up oxygenation and animal origins.

## Active Recall

- What conditions made early Earth different from today?
- What counts as evidence of early life, and why is it hard to interpret?
- How does the origin of life prepare the way for later environmental change?
