Source PDF: `source.pdf`

## Role In The Course

Uses the Carboniferous as a case study in climate, sea level, plants, carbon burial, oxygen, and ecosystems.

## Lecture Goals

- Know the Carboniferous overview.
- Understand coal swamp environments.
- Know coal swamp biotas and their Earth-system consequences.

## High-Yield Content

- Carboniferous coal swamps formed in wet lowland environments with abundant plant productivity and preservation.
- Organic carbon burial helped draw down CO2 and raise O2.
- High oxygen and wet habitats affected terrestrial arthropods and vertebrates.
- Coal is a stored record of ancient biomass and carbon cycling.
- Coal swamp decline connects to climate drying and Permian environmental transition.

## Study Targets

- Know why coal forms: productivity, waterlogged/anoxic conditions, burial, preservation.
- Connect carbon burial to atmospheric CO2/O2.
- Use Lab 7 for fossil/community examples.
