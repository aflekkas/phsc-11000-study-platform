# Lecture 07: Rocks and the Rock Cycle

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 7  
Exam priority: High

## Role In The Course

Starts the inference-tool block: rocks are records of processes and environments.

## Lecture Goals

- Distinguish major rock types.
- Understand the rock cycle.
- Use rocks as environmental evidence.

## High-Yield Content

- Igneous, sedimentary, and metamorphic rocks form through different processes.
- Sedimentary rocks are especially important for reconstructing surface environments and fossils.
- The rock cycle links weathering, erosion, transport, deposition, burial, melting, and uplift.
- Rock composition and texture can encode formation conditions.
- Correct rock identification underlies lab work and later environmental inference.

## Study Targets

- Review sedimentary vs. igneous vs. metamorphic recognition.
- Know clastic, chemical, and biological sedimentary rocks at a practical level.
- Use this for Lab 5 sedimentary-rock/environment questions.

## Active Recall

- Why are sedimentary rocks especially useful for environmental history?
- How does a rock move through the rock cycle?
