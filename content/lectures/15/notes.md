# Lecture 15: The Rise of Oxygen

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 9  
Exam priority: Very High

## Role In The Course

Explains one of the largest environmental transformations: oxygenic photosynthesis and oxygen accumulation.

## Lecture Goals

- Explain oxygenic photosynthesis.
- Know evidence for early oxygenic photosynthesis.
- Understand oxygen rise in atmosphere and oceans.

## High-Yield Content

- Oxygenic photosynthesis produces O2 from water and CO2 using light.
- Oxygen did not accumulate immediately because reduced sinks consumed it.
- Evidence includes stromatolites/microbial mats, isotopic signatures, red beds, banded iron formations, and redox-sensitive minerals.
- The Great Oxidation-type transition changed atmosphere, oceans, metabolism, and mineral systems.
- Oxygen enabled new ecological and evolutionary possibilities but also created stress for anaerobic life.

## Study Targets

- Know source-sink logic for oxygen accumulation.
- Be able to distinguish production of oxygen from accumulation of oxygen.
- Connect oxygenation to animals, Snowball Earth, and carbon burial.

## Active Recall

- Why can organisms produce oxygen before the atmosphere becomes oxygen-rich?
- What evidence shows changing oxygen levels?
- How did oxygen reshape biological and environmental possibilities?
