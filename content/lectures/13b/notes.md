# Lecture 13b: Earth System Science

Source PDF: `source.pdf`  
Pages: 9  
Exam priority: Very High

## Role In The Course

Pulls earlier inference tools into systems thinking: climate and environments result from interacting reservoirs and feedbacks.

## Lecture Goals

- Understand the Earth-system science approach.
- Know key components of Earth systems: atmosphere, biosphere, geosphere, hydrosphere.
- Use systems thinking to interpret feedbacks and uncertainty.

## High-Yield Content

- Earth surface environments are produced by interacting systems, not one variable.
- Models require explicit inputs, interactions, outputs, uncertainty, and scale choices.
- Atmospheric CO2 is controlled by sources and sinks in the carbon cycle.
- Photosynthesis, respiration, organic burial, weathering, limestone formation, volcanism, and subduction all matter.
- Systems thinking asks whether a variable is a forcing, response, feedback, or proxy.

## Study Targets

- Make a carbon-cycle diagram from memory.
- For every climate event, identify forcings and feedbacks.
- Treat this as the central synthesis lecture for the final.

## Active Recall

- What is the difference between a forcing and a feedback?
- How can the same variable be an input in one model and an output in another?
- How does the carbon cycle connect life, rocks, atmosphere, oceans, and climate?
